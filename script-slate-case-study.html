Summary

The entertainment industry is stuck using spreadsheets to track complex projects when it should have the modern products other industries enjoy. With thousands of screenplays changing hands in Hollywood everyday, Script Slate wants to give executives, producers, and indie filmmakers the user experience they deserve to easily organize material and submissions. That way, they have more time to be creative and make content for everyone to enjoy.

Problems

  1. It's hard to keep an organized archive of material in Hollywood (scripts, short films, and feature films)
  2. It is time consuming to track what material you've sent or received and to whom
  3. Switching between programs to look up contact information is anooying

Solutions

  1. Let users upload scripts and videos in all formats, and store them in one sortable and filterable database
  2. Have a tab dedicated to tracking so users can know where material came from or went to and when it happened
  3. Include a contacts tab so that users can access that information in the same app

Competitive Analysis

To ensure Script Slate could carve out a niche in the cloud market, I examined three competitors: Google Drive, Dropbox, and Evernote. They had many strengths such as the ability to upload multiple kinds of files, good design, and folder-based organization. However, none of them had tracking capabilities for files or incorporated contacts that would greatly enhance the Hollywood workflow.

Lauren H.

29 Y/O aspiring filmmaker from Los Angeles

Goals

  • I want to easily track who I've sent my scripts to
  • I want to send my material to my contacts on the fly
  • I want to be able to access all my material in one central place

Frustrations

  • Sometimes I send the wrong version of a script, and I have to resend the correct one
  • I hate remembering a bunch of passwords for different services
  • Creating script breakdowns for my projects is time-consuming

Ethan C.

28 Y/O development coordinator from New York City

Goals

  • I want to know what scripts came into the company in the last week
  • I want to distribute material to team members easily
  • I want to quickly find any obscure script my bosses need to see

Frustrations

  • I have to remind some people to look at their assigned materials over and over
  • Notes on material can be hard to track or format when they are in other documents
  • Sometimes I forget to follow up on material I've sent out to colleagues
